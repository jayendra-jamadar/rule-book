Game Rules

1. Event Structure

  • The event consists of 4 rounds.
  • The first 3 rounds are 1 hour each, and the final round is 1.5 hours.

2. Country and Company Assignment

  • Each country will be assigned 2 companies.
  • One from a strong sector of the country.
  • One from any sector other than the country’s strong sector.

3. Sectors

  • Defence
  • Healthcare
  • Technology
  • Finance
  • Agriculture

4. Initial Resources

  • Each country will start with a base treasury amount of ₹1 crore and a GDP of ₹100 crores.

5. GDP Influence

  • A country’s GDP will be influenced by various factors including:
    • The performance (profit/loss) of the companies owned.
    • Sector and indigenous bonuses.
    • Any loans taken.
    • Changes to the treasury.

6. Internal Bidding Rounds

  • There will be two internal bidding rounds each lasting 15 minutes.
  • These will take place after Round 1 and Round 3.
  • During these periods, countries may trade the companies they have acquired with each other.

Bidding Rules

1. Company Information Presentation

  • A presentation with detailed information about various companies will be displayed.
  • The information provided will include:
    • Company Name
    • Country of Origin
    • Sector
    • Revenue
    • Base Price
  • Participants must use this data to decide whether to place a bid for the company.

2. Bidding Process

  • The bidding will start at the base price.
  • Bids will increase gradually.
  • If the time limit is exceeded, the last bid within the bidding time will be considered final.

3. Time Allocation for Each Bid

  • Total Duration: 4 minutes
  • 1 minute: Company description
  • 2.5 minutes: Bidding
  • 0.5 minute: Buffer

Details Visible to the User

  • Country Name
  • GDP
  • Treasury
  • Strong Sector
  • Loan Details
  • Sectors of Investment: Displayed in the form of a pie chart.

Options Available to the User

Loan Management

  • Loan Allotment and Repayment:
  • User can apply for or repay a loan.
  • An interest (simple interest) of 12% will be deducted after every 5 bids and 1/10 of the remaining principal will also be deducted.
  • Example:
    • Loan amount: ₹1000
    • First installment: ₹100 (principal) + ₹120 (interest) = ₹220
    • Remaining principal: ₹900
    • Second installment: ₹90 (principal) + ₹108 (interest) = ₹198

Foreign Exchange Management

  • Exchange Rates & Repayments:
  • User may buy or sell foreign currency.
  • In the case of purchase, 2% of the transacted amount will be deducted from the user’s account and transferred to the country from which the currency is being bought.
